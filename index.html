Background & roles

Originally from Athens, Greece, I am completing my B.S. in Computer Engineering at Maryland with a focus on ASIC/FPGA and architecture. I serve as Technical Coordinator for the Electronics Prototyping Lab at Terrapin Works, as an Undergraduate Teaching Fellow for Digital Circuits and Systems (ENEE205), and as a Resident Assistant at Easton Hall. I will begin my Ph.D. at UMD next semester, concentrating on hardware security and multidimensional architectures; this summer I join NVIDIA as an ASIC Floorplanning Intern. My long-term aim is to push high-performance computing forward through novel architecture and strong backend implementation. Outside the lab, I follow motorsports closely and am training for my private pilot’s license—another way I engage with precision, systems, and risk management.

How I work

I assume someone else may inherit whatever I ship: run the tests, show the numbers, and keep docs aligned with the repo. If I cannot explain a trade-off, I go back until I can. That applies whether the deliverable is a placer, an SoC block, or firmware.
